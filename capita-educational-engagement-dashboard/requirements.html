Project and Client Background



Our client, CAPITA, is a company that creates software that enables education institutions to improve the quality of education that they provide. Our project, the CAPITA Educational Engagement Dashboard, is a system that automatically gathers and processes engagement information about UK Educational Institutions, from various sources, such as Facebook or Twitter, which will later on benefit our client, and, consequentially, the institutions by improving the education they provide.

Requirements gathering

The initial requirements of the system were set during our first meeting with the client. We were tasked with building a tool that gathers data from social media platforms; analyses the data; and produces some output that can be further used by the company.

For more detailed specifications, we had a face-to-face meeting with our client. Possible solutions and technologies were discussed during the meeting, and upon agreement between us and the client: we waited for a document containing all the final specifications. The delivery of the document was delayed by two weeks due to CAPITA internal problems and, consequently, our project starting date as well. We consider this as an important factor in our working progress, as we had only a general perspective over the project during the first three weeks and we could not start the research on the technologies required.




Personas

Our API is specially designed for future use by developers, as it can be integrated in future technologies. However, the final product may also be used by employees of CAPITA such as marketing teams or product managers that want to make use of the current information for their future product-related decisions.

Name: Thomas Bryan
Type: Marketer
Personal view: Thomas is currently working for a company that provides educational software for educational institutions. His main professional concern is to find fresh ways to deliver information to the potential customers. For this, he needs a variety of sources to pull out information from, including social media, but manually reading and understanding this quantity of data can be daunting.

Name: Daniel Miller
Type: Product Manager
Personal view:Daniel is an experienced product manager who is currently working for a company that offers an effective combination of customer management and deep product knowledge to provide professional support services. His main job is to help his customers by driving product recommendations and sharing insights about general trends in education. For this, he needs to understand their necessities so he is currently spending a lot of time talking to schools and colleges across the UK regions which is difficult and tiring. Hence, a system that automatically gathers and processes all this education-related information would save him a lot of time and energy.
