Project and Client Background



Our client, CAPITA, creates software that enables education institutions to help students achieve their goals. The core aim of our project is to produce a system that automatically gathers and processes engagement information about schools and colleges, from many different sources, in a way that allows these institutions to improve the education they provide.

Requirements gathering

We consider that this is a core process for a smooth development of a complex system, hence, we carefuly paid attention that there won't be any future misunderstandings after this process is complete. It is essential for us to know exactly what our client aims to obtain from our services, reason for which this process took more than expected.

Our first contact with our client was through e-mail, when we got a brief explanation of the status of this project at the specific time and a small overview of the expected requirements. At that time, CAPITA had an experimental approach for this project. As they always want to improve their services, they need to constantly test and develop new technologies aimed for this.

Through that e-mail we met our main stakeholder - Scott Bell - and his first description of the project: a system that gathers data from social media platforms, analyses the data and produces some output that can be further used by the company. However, the description was as general as it sounded, so we had to set up a meeting to start negociating the specifications.

We had a one hour face-to-face meeting that gave Scott the opportunity to walk us through the vision of this project. Hece, we had the chance to discuss possible solutions and technologies for achieving the desired output: a complete social-media perspective for every educational institution in UK.

After we had our meeting, we agreed that Scott will soon provide us a final document that will contain all the specifications - considering the debate that we had on the possible technologies. However, our client did not manage to provide us the document in a short period of time wich caused us a two-weeks delay. We consider that this was an important factor over our working progress, as we only had a general perspective over the project, but we could not start doing the needing research for the technologies that followed to be used.

After three weeks from the starting date of the project, we managed to obtain a final version of the Specification Document, which allowed us started working on the project.





Personas

Altough our API is especially designed for future use by developers, in order to be integrated in future technologies, we are aware that even its current output might be used by some individuals: people from marketing teams or product managers that can make use of the current information for their future product-related decisions.

Name: Thomas Bryan
Type: Marketer
Personal view: Thomas is currently working for a company that provides educational software for educational institutions. His main professional concern is to find fresh ways to deliver information to the potential customers, but first, he needs to discover their necessities. For this, he needs a variety of sources to pull out information from, including social media. However, social media can be a powerful tool to use, but also difficult and tiring to analyze.

Name: Daniel Miller
Type: Product Manager
Personal view:Daniel is currently working for a company that offers an effective combination of customer management and deep product knowledge to provide professional support services. His main job is to help his customers by driving product recommendations and sharing insights about general trends in education. He spends a lot of time talking to diverse schools across the UK regions, independent school sector and international customers. However, what would benefit him more is to discover patterns by gathering up education-related information from social media.
